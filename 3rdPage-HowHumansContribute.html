How Humans Contribute

Hazardous Humans

Several people in our world don't realize that their actions can and will have negative impacts on future generations. Specifically speaking, many humans are depleting the ozone layer and are also creating large amounts of ground-level ozone. Ozone depletion in the stratosphere is a major problem that is often disregarded, and humans are the only one to blames for this declination of ozone. Natural causes (sun spots) have been known to only decrease 1%-2% of the ozone layer, however over 90% of depletion has been connected to ozone depleting substances (ODS) created by human activities. The chemicals chlorine and bromine (from manmade substances such as chlorofluorocarbons) break down ozone, and they also remains in the atmosphere for long periods of time. Chlorofluorocarbons are often released into the stratosphere from aerosol cans, refrigerators, packing material, and cleaning agents. On the bright side, CFCs have been banned. But on the dark side, they still destroy the ozone layer. In fact, the ozone layer over Australia has thinned, allowing harmful radiation to reach the surface of Earth. In addition, ozone holes have formed over Antarctica. To continue, ground-level ozone only forms when other substances created by humans react with normal ozone. Nitrogen oxides and volatile organic compounds are the key ingredients in making "bad" ozone. Nitrogen oxides are mostly released in the form of smog. As the population increases, more and more cars are being used, ultimately creating more nitrogen oxides. VOCs, short for volatile organic compounds, are found in several products.
